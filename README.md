## Introduction

Investing is no longer just about maximizing returns. Today, many investors also consider how companies impact the environment, society, and corporate governance. This approach is known as **ESG Investing**.

As climate change, social responsibility, and corporate transparency become increasingly important, ESG investing has emerged as one of the fastest-growing investment strategies worldwide.

---

## What is ESG?

**ESG** stands for:

* 🌱 **Environmental** – How a company manages its impact on nature, such as carbon emissions, renewable energy, and waste management.
* 👥 **Social** – How a company treats employees, customers, suppliers, and local communities.
* 🏛️ **Governance** – How a company is managed, including board independence, ethics, executive compensation, and shareholder rights.

---

## Why Does ESG Matter?

Companies with strong ESG practices may be better prepared to manage long-term risks such as:

* Climate change
* Regulatory changes
* Reputation risk
* Employee retention
* Corporate governance issues

For investors, ESG is one of many factors that can help evaluate a company's long-term sustainability.

---

## Advantages of ESG Investing

* Encourages sustainable business practices
* Focuses on long-term value creation
* Helps identify non-financial risks
* Aligns investments with personal values

---

## Challenges

ESG investing also has limitations:

* Different ESG rating methodologies
* Limited historical data in some markets
* Potential for "greenwashing"
* No guarantee of higher investment returns

---

## Key Takeaways

* ESG stands for Environmental, Social, and Governance.
* It combines financial analysis with sustainability considerations.
* ESG investing continues to grow globally and in India.
* Investors should use ESG alongside traditional financial analysis.

---

## Final Thoughts

Sustainable investing is becoming an important part of modern finance. While ESG should not be the only factor considered when making investment decisions, understanding ESG concepts can help investors make more informed, long-term decisions.
